Designing a brutalist website

27 April 2020

Websites have been slowly shifting towards minimalistic design for a while now. At the first glance, one might say that designing such a website is beyond easy. In practice, however, what lies beneath those simple websites are hours of fine-tuning and harmonizing everything together.

I've kept simplicity in mind from the very beginning of designing this blog. I fondly looked up to Brutalist Web Design, which for me was the perfect minimalist website. I've always been a huge fan of brutalist design in architecture and I wanted my website to reflect this.

After scrolling through sources of the aforementioned website, I've decided to use a Tachyons framework and I've immediately fallen in love with it. It gave me freedom similar to self-written CSS while keeping my components in harmony - all without much thinking. Using Tachyons saved me plenty of time, since now instead of fine-tuning my variables to every display, I just dropped class="pa3 center measure-wide lh-copy tj" on my div and called it a day (slight exaggeration).

A few hours later, I present to you - my minimalistic blog. I hope you enjoy the content here as much as I enjoy writing it.
